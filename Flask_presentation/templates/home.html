About the Project

Analyzing Concentration of Unemployment in US states and counties

In this final project our team decided to analyze unemployment data for US states and their counties. We wanted to see which states and counties has the most unemployment. The question we hope to answer is 'which state/county has the most unemployed population'. Using unemployment and demographic data to predict the unemployment for those states and counties.

The BLS data shows that the nation's three largest cities LA, New York and Chicago have unemployment rates dramatically higher than the 2020 national average of 5.9 percent. The reason we choose this topic is our curiosity about the “great resignation”, and what percentage of labor force is actually unemployed and not contributing to the country’s economy.

Overall the outline of this analysis is getting employment ,demographic data and education data, clean ,merged before uploading the data into a database and used in a machine model, visualizations and interactive maps to see the results.

Data-exploration and analysis:

Data exploration is the first step of data analysis used to explore and visualize data to uncover insights from the start or identify areas and patterns to investigate. After cleaning and uploading the data into our database, time is spent exploring data and noticing trends in the data. Trying to identify what data and analysis will answer our question best and what story can be told from the dataset. Using pandas and matplotlib to analyze the dataset, we started to see trends that counties with most population also have more unemployment. Then we wanted to see if education levels can influence unemployment rates. Education data was added to the visualization and machine learning model to help identify additional relationship between population, education, and unemployment. Finally, visualization of our data can be found on the analysis page.

Machine Learning:

Data preprocessing improves the data quality by cleaning, normalizing, transforming, and extracting relevant feature from raw data. Our raw data came in with columns with data type objects, the machine model we have built can only take in columns with numbers, we have formatted the dataframe “features” into integer columns, dropped unwanted columns, remove null/missing values. Feature engineering is the process of selecting, manipulating, and transforming raw data into features that can be used in supervised learning. To make our model work well, we have discussed which features should be included and which one should be dropped. For accurate predictions the features should be picked carefully, however in our dataset unemployment will be more where there is higher population, so we decided to add education information as a feature as well. With a test score of 93%, so our random regressor model is performing well and not overfitting.
